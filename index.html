Action

Next Steps

1
Improve the abandoned checkout flow

Add a second reminder email with a different angle — urgency, social proof, or a specific product. Review timing so the sequence fits within a short sale window.

Do now
2
Track online-to-showroom visits

Add "How did you find us?" at POS or a trackable bring-in code. Real ecommerce ROI is likely higher than Shopify shows.

Do now
3
Pre-select hero products before next sale

Byron Sofa drove 22% of revenue because it was in the room and in the ads. Make this a deliberate call every time — brief Cande before campaign planning.

Before next sale
4
Send launch email 3–5 days earlier

The teaser had 45% open rate — the audience is ready. Earlier sends give buyers time to plan a showroom visit before the sale opens.

Before next sale
5
Review and strengthen the post-purchase flow

30 new customers acquired. Review the existing flow — is it landing? Could it do more? Think about a specific re-engagement action: a seasonal nudge, a complementary product, or a showroom invitation 3–6 months later.

Before next sale
6
Keep improving product photography

AI-enhanced photos were a lever this sale. Continue refining — prioritise top-selling SKUs and hero products planned for the next campaign.

Ongoing
7
Continue SEO, AEO and GEO work

Organic search brought 738 sessions and 4 orders during the sale. Sustained investment in search and AI-cited content builds a channel that works without ad spend.

Ongoing
8
Evaluate hiding products that have never sold online

The full catalogue is intentional — it works as a digital showroom that drives in-store visits. But it's worth testing: do low-converting products create friction that hurts the buyers who are actually ready to buy online? A controlled test could answer this without committing to a full cull.
